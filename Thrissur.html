TOP DESTINATIONS

Athirapally Waterfall

Athirapally Waterfall is a breathtaking cascade that stands as one of the largest and most majestic waterfalls in the country. Nestled amidst the lush greenery of the Western Ghats, the waterfall is formed by the Chalakudy River as it descends from the Anamudi mountains. The sheer force and beauty of Athirapally attract nature lovers, photographers, and tourists alike, making it a popular destination in Kerala. The surrounding landscape is rich in biodiversity, and the area is part of the Sholayar Range, contributing to its scenic charm Visitors to Athirapally can enjoy not only the mesmerizing views of the waterfall but also the vibrant flora and fauna of the surrounding Athirapally Vazhachal Forest Range. The waterfall is surrounded by dense tropical forests, providing a habitat for various species of plants, animals, and birds. The area has become a favored location for film shoots, capturing the enchanting beauty of Athirapally on the silver screen. With its natural splendor and tranquil ambiance, Athirapally Waterfall offers a serene escape and an opportunity to immerse oneself in the pristine beauty of Kerala's landscape.

Vadakkunnathan Temple

Vadakkunnathan Temple, located in the heart of Thrissur city in Kerala, India, is an ancient and revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Believed to be one of the oldest temples in the state, Vadakkunnathan Temple is a classic example of Kerala's traditional temple architecture. The temple complex is surrounded by a massive stone wall and features a Koothambalam (temple theater) with exquisite wood carvings, adding to its cultural and historical significance. The main deity of the temple is Lord Shiva in the form of Vadakkunnathan, meaning the Lord of the North. The temple's sanctum sanctorum houses a sacred Shiva Lingam, and devotees from far and wide visit to offer prayers and seek blessings. The annual festival, Thrissur Pooram, held at the temple, is a grand celebration that attracts crowds with its elaborate processions, traditional music, and vibrant fireworks. Vadakkunnathan Temple is not only a religious center but also a symbol of Kerala's rich architectural and cultural heritage, drawing visitors with its spiritual aura and timeless charm.

Shakthan Thampuran Palace

Shakthan Thampuran Palace, located in the heart of Thrissur in Kerala, India, is a historic marvel that reflects the grandeur of a bygone era. Built in the traditional Kerala-Dutch style in the 18th century, the palace served as the residence of the Maharaja of Cochin, Rama Varma Shakthan Thampuran. The architectural brilliance of the palace is evident in its spacious halls, intricately designed windows, and wooden carvings that showcase a blend of indigenous and European influences. The palace has been converted into a museum, providing visitors with a glimpse into the royal history of the region. The museum exhibits a rich collection of artifacts, including antique furniture, sculptures, coins, manuscripts, and royal memorabilia. One of the notable features is the preserved sword and dagger of the legendary warrior king, Shakthan Thampuran. The palace's surroundings include well-maintained gardens, enhancing the overall charm of the site. Shakthan Thampuran Palace stands as a testament to the architectural and cultural heritage of Kerala, inviting tourists and history enthusiasts to explore its regal past and appreciate the craftsmanship of a bygone era.

Our Lady of Dolours Basilica

Our Lady of Dolours Basilica, commonly known as Puthanpally, is a prominent Roman Catholic basilica located in Thrissur, Kerala, India. Constructed in the Gothic architectural style, the basilica is renowned for its majestic structure and religious significance. The foundation stone of the church was laid in 1929, and the basilica was consecrated in 1940. The impressive twin towers, soaring spires, and intricate detailing make it a distinctive landmark in the city. basilica is dedicated to the Our Lady of Dolours (Sorrow), and it attracts pilgrims and tourists alike with its spiritual ambiance and artistic beauty. The interior of the basilica is adorned with beautiful stained glass windows, depicting biblical scenes and religious motifs. The annual Feast of Our Lady of Dolours, celebrated in the month of November, is a grand event that draws thousands of devotees from different parts of the country. Our Lady of Dolours Basilica is not only a place of worship but also a symbol of architectural excellence and cultural heritage, contributing to the religious diversity and rich history of Thrissur.
