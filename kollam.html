TOP DESTINATIONS

Ashtamudi Lake

Ashtamudi Lake is an enchanting and expansive water body renowned for its unique octopus-shaped configuration. Covering an area of about 30 square kilometers, it is the second-largest lake in Kerala. The name "Ashtamudi" translates to "eight coned," referring to the eight arms or channels that radiate from the central lake, creating a picturesque and intricate network of waterways.Ashtamudi Lake is celebrated for its serene backwaters, lush green landscapes, and traditional houseboat cruises that offer a leisurely journey through the scenic beauty of Kerala's backwater region. The lake is also known for the coir industry, and visitors can witness the traditional coir-making process in the nearby villages. The shores of Ashtamudi are dotted with quaint villages, where one can experience the local way of life, visit ancient temples, and savor the delectable Kerala cuisine. With its tranquil waters, vibrant local culture, and opportunities for boating and birdwatching, Ashtamudi Lake presents a captivating blend of natural beauty and cultural richness in the heart of Kerala.

Jatayu Earth's Centre

Jatayu Earth's Centre, situated in the southern state of Kerala, India, is a unique and innovative tourism destination that combines myth, nature, and adventure. Named after Jatayu, a mythical eagle from the Indian epic Ramayana, the site boasts the world's largest bird sculpture, a monumental representation of Jatayu perched atop a hill. TThe colossal sculpture, measuring over 200 feet in length and 150 feet in width, serves as a symbolic representation of the legendary bird's attempt to rescue Sita, the abducted consort of Lord Rama. The site offers not only a cultural and mythological experience but also features a range of adventure activities, including rock climbing and rappelling. The hill also provides panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes, making it a popular spot for tourists and trekkers. Jatayu Earth's Centre aims to blend mythology, nature, and adventure tourism, providing visitors with a unique and immersive experience in the lush and picturesque terrain of Kerala.

Thenmala Ecotourism

Thenmala Ecotourism Project is a pioneering initiative that seamlessly blends nature conservation with responsible tourism practices. Aptly named "Thenmala," meaning "Honey Hill," the destination is a haven for eco-enthusiasts, offering a harmonious combination of lush landscapes, diverse wildlife, and sustainable development. The project features well-planned trekking trails, boating experiences on the Shenduruney Wildlife Sanctuary Lake, and a unique Butterfly Safari, providing visitors with an immersive connection to the natural beauty of the region.What sets Thenmala apart is its commitment to sustainable tourism, focusing on environmental conservation and community involvement. The park showcases India's first rock-cut garden adorned with sculptures and mural paintings, contributing to the cultural and historical richness of the destination. As a model for eco-friendly tourism, Thenmala not only provides a rejuvenating retreat for travelers but also serves as an inspiring example of how responsible tourism can coexist with the preservation of natural ecosystems.

Thangassery Lighthouse

The Tangasseri Lighthouse, located in the coastal town of Tangasseri in Kollam, Kerala, is a historic beacon that stands as a testament to the maritime heritage of the region. Dating back to the 20th century, this iconic structure served as a vital aid to navigation for ships entering the Kollam Port. The lighthouse is perched on the picturesque Tangasseri Cliff, offering breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea and the surrounding landscapes. Its distinctive red and white stripes add to the visual allure of this architectural marvel.Beyond its practical navigational function, the Tangasseri Lighthouse is a cultural landmark, attracting visitors with its historical significance. The lighthouse compound includes a museum that showcases artifacts and exhibits related to the maritime history of Kollam. Visitors can climb to the top of the lighthouse for panoramic views of the coastline, making it a popular destination for both history enthusiasts and those seeking a scenic vantage point. The Tangasseri Lighthouse serves as a beacon not only for maritime navigation but also as a beacon for those interested in Kerala's coastal history and the mesmerizing beauty of the Arabian Sea.
