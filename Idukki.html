TOP DESTINATIONS

Munnar

Munnar is a picturesque hill station nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India, renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, lush tea plantations, and cool climate. Perched at an altitude of around 1,600 meters above sea level, Munnar offers a refreshing escape from the tropical heat with its mist-covered hills and verdant valleys. The region is adorned with sprawling tea estates that paint the hillsides in vibrant shades of green, creating a captivating panorama. Apart from its natural beauty, Munnar also boasts diverse flora and fauna, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, and indulge in activities like trekking, bird watching, and enjoying the tranquility of its serene lakes and waterfalls.In addition to its natural attractions, Munnar features charming colonial architecture, with tea plantation bungalows and churches that reflect its historical past. The town is dotted with tea museums, showcasing the evolution of the tea industry in the region. The vibrant local culture, warm hospitality, and the aroma of freshly brewed tea add to the unique charm of Munnar, making it a must-visit destination for those seeking a peaceful retreat amidst nature's splendor.

Idukki Arch Dam

The Idukki Arch Dam, located in the southern Indian state of Kerala, stands as an engineering marvel and a key landmark in the region. Completed in 1975, this colossal dam is built across the Periyar River in the picturesque district of Idukki. It is renowned as one of the tallest arch dams in Asia, soaring to a height of about 168 meters and stretching over 365 meters in length. The Idukki Arch Dam not only serves as a vital water reservoir for irrigation and power generation but also offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding hills and the Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary. The reservoir created by the dam, known as Idukki Reservoir, is flanked by lush greenery and is a popular destination for boating. The dam's architectural grandeur, coupled with the scenic beauty of the Western Ghats, attracts both engineering enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. The dam plays a crucial role in supporting the region's agriculture, hydroelectric power generation, and contributes to Kerala's unique landscape, making it a noteworthy attraction in the state.

Thekkady

Thekkady is a captivating destination known for its rich biodiversity and the famous Periyar National Park. Nestled in the Western Ghats, Thekkady is renowned for its lush greenery, dense forests, and a picturesque lake that adds to its natural charm. The heart of Thekkady is the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, offering a unique opportunity for wildlife enthusiasts to explore the diverse flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, and a variety of bird species. Visitors can enjoy a boat cruise on the Periyar Lake, providing a tranquil setting to witness the beauty of the sanctuary and spot wildlife along the water's edge. Apart from its wildlife attractions, Thekkady is surrounded by spice plantations, and visitors can take guided tours to learn about the cultivation of spices like cardamom, pepper, and cinnamon. The town also offers opportunities for trekking, bamboo rafting, and other outdoor activities, making it a well-rounded destination for those seeking a blend of nature, adventure, and cultural experiences in the heart of Kerala's Western Ghats.

Periyar National Park

Periyar National Park, situated in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India, stands as a testament to the region's extraordinary biodiversity and conservation efforts. The park, encompassing the picturesque Periyar Lake, is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. One of the key attractions is the Periyar Tiger Reserve, home to a diverse array of fauna, including elephants, tigers, leopards, deer, and numerous bird species. Visitors to Periyar National Park can embark on guided boat tours on the placid Periyar Lake, providing a unique opportunity to observe wildlife in their natural habitat along the shores. The park also offers trekking and nature walk experiences, allowing adventurers to explore the dense evergreen and deciduous forests, enhancing the overall experience of being immersed in the lap of nature. The ecotourism initiatives in the park contribute to the conservation of the Western Ghats ecosystem, making Periyar National Park not only a remarkable destination for wildlife enthusiasts but also a model for sustainable and responsible tourism in India.
