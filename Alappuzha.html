TOP DESTINATIONS

Alappuzha Beach

Alappuzha Beach, also known as Alleppey Beach, is a picturesque coastal stretch situated along the Arabian Sea in the southern Indian state of Kerala. Famous for its golden sands, gentle waves, and a charming lighthouse, the beach is a popular destination that attracts both locals and tourists alike. The 150-year-old Alappuzha Lighthouse stands tall at the beach, offering panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and the surrounding town. In addition to its natural beauty, Alappuzha Beach is known for hosting various cultural events and festivals, making it a vibrant hub for local festivities. The beach provides a tranquil setting for leisurely strolls, beach volleyball, and other recreational activities. The presence of traditional Kerala houseboats and the historic pier adds to the coastal charm, making Alappuzha Beach a multifaceted destination where visitors can appreciate the natural beauty, engage in cultural experiences, and unwind along the serene coastline of Kerala.

Alappuzha Lighthouse

The Alappuzha Lighthouse, standing proudly on the Alappuzha Beach in the southern Indian state of Kerala, is a historic landmark that adds to the coastal allure of the region. Built in 1862, this 30-meter tall lighthouse offers not only a striking architectural presence but also panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and the surrounding landscape. Its red and white striped exterior, typical of traditional lighthouse aesthetics, makes it an iconic structure against the backdrop of the golden sands and blue waters. Marari Beach

Marari Beach is a pristine and tranquil coastal haven renowned for its serene beauty and laid-back atmosphere. Stretching along the Arabian Sea, Marari offers visitors a respite from the hustle and bustle of city life with its soft golden sands, swaying coconut palms, and the soothing rhythm of the waves. Unlike some of the more crowded beaches in the region, Marari retains a peaceful and uncrowded charm, making it an ideal retreat for those seeking relaxation and rejuvenation. The beach is named after the quaint fishing village of Mararikulam, where the local way of life blends seamlessly with the natural beauty of the surroundings. Marari Beach is dotted with traditional fishing boats, and visitors can witness the daily routines of the local fishermen, adding an authentic touch to the experience. The area offers a range of activities, from beachside yoga sessions and Ayurvedic spa treatments to exploring the nearby backwaters on houseboats. Whether it's unwinding in the hammocks under the swaying palms, taking leisurely walks along the shore, or enjoying the fresh catch of the day at local eateries, Marari Beach promises a tranquil and immersive coastal experience in the heart of God's Own Country.

Punnamada Lake

Punnamada Lake is a breathtaking expanse of backwaters renowned for its serene beauty and cultural significance. The lake is a vital part of the intricate network of waterways that make up the famous Kerala backwaters. Known for hosting the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, Punnamada Lake attracts visitors from around the world who gather to witness the exhilarating snake boat races that showcase the traditional prowess and competitive spirit of the local communities.The tranquil waters of Punnamada Lake are surrounded by lush green landscapes, coconut groves, and picturesque villages, creating a mesmerizing backdrop for houseboat cruises. Travelers can embark on a leisurely journey through the backwaters, allowing them to experience the unique lifestyle of the region while enjoying the peaceful ambiance. The lake is not only a visual delight but also plays a crucial role in the local economy, supporting traditional activities like fishing and coir manufacturing. Punnamada Lake stands as a symbol of Kerala's backwater charm, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural vibrancy, and immersive experiences for those exploring the enchanting landscapes of the region.
