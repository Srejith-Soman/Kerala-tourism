TOP DESTINATIONS

Mini Ooty

Arimbra Hills, often referred to as "Mini Ooty," stands as a popular tourist destination between Malappuram and Kondotty in Kerala, India. With an elevation of 445 meters above sea level, it captivates visitors with rolling hills, scenic landscapes, and a resemblance to the famous Ooty hill station. The area is dotted with stone crushers, plantations, and hosts an old Scheduled Caste colony on its western side.Within Kerala's Malappuram district, Arimbra mountain in Kondotty is the third-highest among five major independent mountains. Cheriyam mountain (Panthalur Hill) holds the top position at 613 meters, followed by Mount Amminikkadan at 540 meters. Arimbra mountain adds to the natural allure of the region, attracting a significant number of visitors seeking panoramic views and a serene escape.

Nilambur teak Museum

The Nilambur Teak Museum is a unique institution dedicated to showcasing the rich history and significance of teak cultivation in the region. The museum, established by the Kerala Forest Research Institute, provides a comprehensive insight into the teak industry, particularly highlighting Nilambur's role as the world's first teak plantation. The museum's exhibits include historical artifacts, photographs, and informative displays that trace the evolution of teak cultivation and its economic and cultural impact on the region. Visitors to the Nilambur Teak Museum can explore the various stages of teak wood processing, understand the lifecycle of teak trees, and learn about the sustainable practices employed in the industry. The museum is surrounded by lush greenery, creating a tranquil environment that complements the educational experience. It serves as a valuable resource for researchers, students, and anyone interested in forestry, showcasing the significance of teak in Kerala's heritage and economy.

Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary

Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary is a breathtaking sanctuary that unfolds at the convergence of the Kadalundi River and the Arabian Sea. This haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts showcases a dynamic interplay of habitats, including mudflats, tidal ponds, and mangrove forests, providing an ideal environment for an extensive variety of avian species. The sanctuary is especially renowned for its role as a migratory hotspot, attracting birds from distant regions during the migratory season, and it has become a crucial site for the conservation of diverse birdlife.Boasting residents such as sandpipers, gulls, terns, and herons, the Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary offers a serene escape into nature. The estuarine landscape, coupled with the vibrant avian population, underscores the significance of preserving such ecosystems for environmental balance. The sanctuary not only serves as a vital refuge for birds but also as a peaceful retreat for visitors seeking to witness the beauty of Kerala's natural biodiversity in a tranquil and undisturbed setting.

Kodikuthimala

Kodikuthimala is a scenic hill station that has gained popularity for its breathtaking landscapes and natural beauty. Nestled amid lush greenery and undulating hills, Kodikuthimala offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, making it a serene retreat for nature enthusiasts and those seeking a peaceful getaway.The hill station is characterized by its vast expanses of grassy meadows, making it an ideal destination for picnics and outdoor activities. Visitors can indulge in trekking, nature walks, and photography, capturing the stunning vistas that unfold from various vantage points. The tranquil ambiance and cool climate contribute to the allure of Kodikuthimala, providing a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of urban life.
